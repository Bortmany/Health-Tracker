# Approval workflow — the Notion contract

Board: **Marketing Queue** (Notion database). One card = one piece of content. Statuses:

`Drafted` → needs a graphic or another agent's step; not ready for the owner yet
`Awaiting approval` → complete; owner's move
`Approved` → **owner-only status.** No agent may ever set it. Publisher acts on these.
`Scheduled` / `Published` → publisher sets after acting, with the live link
`Rejected` → owner said no; agent moves content to `drafts/rejected/` WITH the owner's comment, and learns from it

Card fields: Name · Status · Project (Cut/TradeOS/Bean & Brew/Oman Property/Dukkani) · Platform (Instagram/X/Reddit/Email/Ad/Report/Meta Ads/Google Ads/TikTok Ads) · Type (Post/Reply/Email/Calendar/Proposal/Digest/Campaign/Experiment) · Publish date · Why (one line). Body: the full copy, image, thread link for Reddit replies, Canva link.

Campaign cards carry three extra fields: Budget cap, Kill threshold, Experiment ID. For a Campaign card, dragging to Approved means "I will press the platform buttons exactly as the card says" — the buttons themselves are always the owner's.

Owner interaction model: drag to Approved = ship it as-is; comment without moving = revise (copywriter picks it up next run); drag to Rejected = drop it and learn. Nothing expires silently — stale cards get surfaced in the Thursday report.
