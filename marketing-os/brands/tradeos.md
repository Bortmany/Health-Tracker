# Brand profile: TradeOS

## Product
- **What it is:** A trading discipline platform for futures day traders — a trading journal where the core feature is *enforcement*, not note-taking. Import trades (CSV from TopstepX/Tradovate/NinjaTrader/Rithmic/IBKR, or live auto-sync via the TopstepX API), define personal trading rules in a no-code builder ("only trade 9:30–11:30", "max 3 contracts", "no revenge trades"), and every trade is automatically graded pass/fail against them, rolled into an explainable 0–100 discipline score. Plus: full analytics (expectancy, profit factor, performance by session/time-of-day/strategy) and a prop-firm tracker showing live buffer remaining on trailing drawdown, daily loss limits, and consistency rules — *before* the breach.
- **Scope:** futures first (ES/NQ/MES/MNQ etc.). NOT stocks, crypto, or options right now — never imply otherwise.
- **Pricing:** Free tier · Pro $29/mo · Elite $79/mo · 14-day full trial, no card required.
- **Platform:** web app, mobile PWA.
- **Stage:** owner describes a complete product; the TradeOS repo in this workspace is empty — CONFIRM WITH OWNER what is live and where before any copy says "available now" or links out. Until confirmed, copy sells the idea and the problem, not availability.
- **Optimizing for:** trial signups (once live-status is confirmed); audience/waitlist until then.

## Audience
- **Primary:** funded and prop-firm evaluation traders — Topstep, Apex, Take Profit Trader combines. Already paying $150+/mo in eval fees. Their #1 problem is blowing accounts by breaking rules (daily loss limits, trailing drawdown, revenge trading) — NOT lacking a strategy.
- **Secondary:** serious retail futures day traders who know their setup works but can't stay consistent.
- **Explicitly NOT for:** casual investors, buy-and-hold, beginners wanting signals/picks. There is no advice, no signals, no AI black box — everything deterministic and auditable. Say this proudly; it's a differentiator, not a disclaimer.
- **The emotional hook (owner's words — this is the campaign):** "You don't lose money because your setup is bad. You lose it because you break your own rules — TradeOS makes that measurable."

## Voice
- **Tone:** straightforward, numbers-literate, calm. The trader who survived, not the guru selling the dream. Credibility is the entire game.
- **We always:** talk cost-of-indiscipline in concrete numbers; respect that the audience is skeptical of every trading product (they should be); show mechanics ("here's exactly how the score is computed") — auditability IS the brand.
- **We never:** give financial advice or trade ideas, post signals, promise profits or eval passes ("pass your combine guaranteed" is banned), flex P&L, use rocket/moon/get-rich language, trash-talk prop firms (their rules are the terrain, not the enemy — factual only when naming firms).

## Content pillars
1. **Discipline, measured** — revenge trading, tilt, the real dollar cost of breaking your own rules; psychology made quantifiable
2. **Prop-firm survival** — trailing drawdown math, consistency rules, why evals actually fail; genuinely useful even without the product
3. **What your data says** — expectancy, profit factor, time-of-day effects; "your edge has office hours"
4. **Build-in-public / how it works** — the rule engine, how grading works, transparency as content
5. **Anti-hype** — no signals, no gurus, no black box; counterprogramming against trading-influencer noise
